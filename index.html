About me

I am a chemical engineer by training, with a PhD in GeoEnergy Engineering from Heriot-Watt University and BSc/MSc degrees in Petroleum Engineering from Sharif University of Technology. My research focuses on understanding and modelling coupled thermo–hydro–chemical processes in complex porous and process systems, from pore-scale physics to continuum-scale behaviour.

Methodologically, I combine advanced experiments (microfluidics, X-ray micro-CT, MRI, μPIV and fluorescence imaging) with numerical modelling (lattice Boltzmann, pore-network modelling, CFD) and data-driven approaches to address challenges in carbon capture, utilisation and storage (CCUS), hydrogen storage, gas hydrates, and subsurface energy engineering.

I enjoy working at the interface of disciplines and collaborating with academic, industrial and synchrotron partners to translate fundamental understanding into impact for the energy transition.

Research

My research sits at the intersection of transport phenomena, multiphase flow, and reactive transport in porous and process systems, with a strong emphasis on problems relevant to the energy transition and net-zero targets.

I develop and couple pore-scale models (lattice Boltzmann, pore-network models), continuum-scale simulations, and advanced imaging to quantify flow and transport in geomaterials, engineered porous media, and multiphase systems. Increasingly, I integrate machine learning and data-driven analysis to extract patterns, accelerate simulations and support upscaling.

Current research directions
  • Porous media for net-zero – CO₂ and H₂ storage, injectivity and formation damage, salt precipitation, and coupled THCM behaviour in saline aquifers and reservoir rocks.
  • Multiphase flow and reactive transport – pore-scale dynamics of drying, precipitation, and non-Fickian transport under unsaturated conditions, and their upscaling to continuum-scale models.
  • Gas hydrates science and technology – hydrate formation/dissociation in porous media, hydrate-based separation processes, and their roles in CCUS, desalination and energy security.
  • Data-driven and ML-enhanced modelling – using convolutional neural networks and surrogate models to predict flow signatures, permeability and transport coefficients from images and simulations.
