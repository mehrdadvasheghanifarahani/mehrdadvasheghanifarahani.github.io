Projects

A selection of ongoing and recent projects I have been involved in, spanning pore-scale modelling, microfluidics, X-ray imaging and net-zero technologies.

PINCH – Performance and Injectivity of CO₂ in Hyper-saline Aquifers

EPSRC-funded · The University of Manchester

As Research Fellow (and previously PDRA), I work on pore-scale modelling of salt precipitation, permeability impairment and multiphase flow in hyper-saline aquifers using lattice Boltzmann simulations coupled with reactive transport.

  • Multiphysics LBM development for salt precipitation.
  • Analysis of injectivity loss under realistic reservoir conditions.
  • Collaboration with Durham, Stuttgart, BP, Equinor and Shell.
4D micro-CT of salt precipitation (Diamond Light Source)

I12–JEEP Beamline · Co-Investigator

Co-Investigator on a Diamond Light Source project developing 4D micro-CT imaging experiments to quantify pore-level salt precipitation and permeability evolution during CO₂ injection into reservoir rocks.

  • Time-resolved imaging of brine drying & salt growth.
  • Pore-scale structure extraction & permeability analysis.
  • Integration with LBM and pore-network simulations.
Contaminant transport and mixing in porous geomaterials

EPSRC Impact Acceleration & RFCS MINRESCUE · University of Warwick

At Warwick, I developed LBM simulations and PDMS microfluidic experiments to characterise contaminant transport, dispersion and mixing, and contributed to the EU MINRESCUE project on recycled mining waste geomaterials.

  • 3D LBM simulations of turbulent & transitional flow.
  • Fluorescent microfluidic imaging of mixing in micromodels.
  • X-ray CT, ML segmentation and pore-network modelling.
Gas hydrates: climate, CCUS and separation processes

Centre for Gas Hydrate Research · Heriot-Watt University

During my PhD and subsequent collaborations I worked on hydrate-bearing sediments, hydrate-based separations, and hydrate slurry flow, using lab experiments and pore-scale modelling tools.

  • Hydrate quantification using coupled geophysical–geothermal schemes.
  • MRI & X-ray imaging of hydrate formation/dissociation.
  • Hydrate thermodynamics & flow assurance modelling (HydraFLASH).
