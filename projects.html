Projects

A selection of ongoing and recent projects I have been involved in, spanning pore-scale modelling, microfluidics and X-ray imaging for porous media and net-zero technologies.

PINCH – Performance and Injectivity of CO₂ in Hyper-saline Aquifers

EPSRC-funded project · Department of Chemical Engineering, The University of Manchester

As Research Fellow and previously Postdoctoral Research Associate, I contribute to a multi-partner EPSRC project on the performance and injectivity of supercritical CO₂ in hyper-saline aquifers. My work focuses on pore-scale modelling of salt precipitation, permeability impairment and multiphase flow in heterogeneous rocks, using lattice Boltzmann simulations coupled with reactive transport models.

  • Development of a multiphysics LBM framework for salt precipitation in porous media.
  • Analysis of injectivity loss due to salt blockage under realistic field conditions.
  • Collaboration with academic partners (Durham, Stuttgart) and industrial partners (BP, Equinor, Shell).
4D micro-CT of salt precipitation (Diamond Light Source)

I12–JEEP beamtime · Co-Investigator

I am Co-Investigator on a Diamond Light Source project using 4D micro-CT imaging to quantify pore-level salt precipitation and permeability impairment induced by CO₂ injection into porous reservoir rocks.   • Time-resolved imaging of brine drying and salt growth in realistic rock cores.
  • Extraction of pore-scale statistics and permeability evolution for upscaling.
  • Integration with LBM and pore-network simulations for model validation.
Contaminant transport and mixing in porous geomaterials

EPSRC Impact Acceleration & RFCS MINRESCUE · University of Warwick

At Warwick, I developed lattice Boltzmann models and PDMS microfluidic experiments to characterise contaminant transport, dispersion and mixing across a wide range of flow regimes. I also contributed to the EU RFCS MINRESCUE project, quantifying pore structure and hydraulic properties of recycled coal-mining waste geomaterials for construction. :contentReference[oaicite:32]{index=32}

  • LBM simulations of turbulent and transitional flows in 3D porous media.
  • Fluorescent microfluidic imaging of mixing and dispersion in 2.5D micromodels.
  • X-ray CT + ML segmentation + pore-network modelling of recycled geomaterials.
Gas hydrates: climate, CCUS and separation processes

Centre for Gas Hydrate Research · Heriot-Watt University

During my PhD and subsequent collaborations, I worked on gas hydrate-bearing permafrost sediments, hydrate-based separations, and hydrate slurry flow, combining lab experiments and pore-scale modelling.

  • Coupled geophysical–geothermal schemes for hydrate quantification in permafrost.
  • MRI and X-ray imaging of hydrate formation/dissociation in porous media.
  • Hydrate thermodynamics and flow assurance modelling with HydraFLASH.
